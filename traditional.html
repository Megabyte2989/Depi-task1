Unveiling Alexandria's Traditional Flavors:

Alexandria, the vibrant Egyptian port city, boasts a rich history and a cuisine just as captivating. Unlike Upper Egypt, Alexandrian cuisine is influenced by its coastal location and the diverse communities that have called it home. Let's embark on a delicious adventure, exploring the unique flavors and dishes that define Alexandria's culinary heritage

Fresh from the Sea:

Given its proximity to the Mediterranean Sea, seafood takes center stage in Alexandrian cuisine. Samak mashwy (grilled whole fish) is a quintessential dish, simply seasoned and cooked to perfection, allowing the natural flavors to shine. For a bolder taste, try samak singari, a local favorite featuring fish cooked in a tomato sauce infused with cilantro, cumin, and chili. Gambari mashwy (grilled prawns) is another must-try, offering a delightful combination of sweet and savory.

A Touch of France:

Alexandria's historical ties with Europe are evident in its Alexandrian cheese fondue. This creamy delight, unlike its Swiss counterpart, is typically enjoyed with pita bread or vegetables, offering a delightful appetizer or light meal

Foul with a Twist:

Foul medames, the national Egyptian dish of mashed fava beans, gets a unique twist in Alexandria. Foul Eskandarani incorporates bell peppers and tomatoes, adding a vibrant touch to this classic breakfast dish

Liver on the Rise:

Kebda Eskandarani (Alexandrian liver) is a local favorite. This dish features fried beef liver seasoned with a symphony of spices like cumin, garlic, cardamom, and chili. It's typically served in sandwiches with tahini dip, on rice, or simply with pita bread on the side.

Sweet Endings:

Alexandria's rich Italian heritage is evident in its gelatee, the local term for ice cream. Many Alexandrian shops boast generations-old recipes, offering a delightful and refreshing way to end your culinary exploration.
